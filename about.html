About Darren

I graduated with a degree in Communication Studies from Nanyang Technological University (NTU), and then proceeded to obtain a Masters at the Royal Melbourne Institute of Technology (RMIT). My research work was largely focused on language and the important role it plays in shaping our lives.

I stuttered since I was about four and struggled to cope with it from my teenage years to early adulthood. I avoided speaking when I knew I would stutter, and this need to hold back what I wanted to say was both upsetting and disappointing because expression is such a basic human desire.

Verbal communication – to be able to say what you wish to say – is usually taken for granted. For stutterers, speaking can be a joy on days they are fluent, and an almost impossible task on bad days, when even ordering a burger at a counter is daunting. As I used to stutter, I can relate to the frustrations and anxiety that stutterers face daily.

I speak fluently now and am comfortable in all kinds of speaking situations – interpersonal, group or public communication. I am also no longer the same person I was when I stuttered. Stuttering has a neurological cause and it is necessary to change our habitual patterns of thought to eradicate stutter – what I call transformation therapy. I believe a stutterer’s personality needs to change in order to get rid of the stutter.

I help stutterers through a multi-pronged approach – combining techniques that can help reduce stutter immediately and a longer term transformational therapy to eradicate stutter.
